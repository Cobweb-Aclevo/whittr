Whittr | Community Guidelines + EULA


Whittr Community Guidelines and End-User license agreement.

We at Whittr believe that communication is vital. It’s amazing to see so many communities and people use this application to converse and Whittr with one another and allow those to find new friends.

The overwhelming majority of people use Whittr responsibly, so these guidelines may seem obvious. Still we want to be clear about the expectations for users.


Here are the rules for our users

Do not organize, participate in, or encourage harassment of others.
Disagreements happen and are normal, but continuous, repetitive, or severe negative comments may cross the line into harassment and are not okay.

Do not organize, promote, or coordinate chats centered around hate speech.
It’s unacceptable to attack a person or a community based on attributes such as their race, ethnicity, national origin, sex, gender, sexual orientation, religious affiliation, or disabilities.

Do not make threats of violence or threaten to harm others.
This includes indirect threats, as well as sharing or threatening to share someone’s private personal information (also known as doxxing).

Do not send others viruses or malware, attempt to phish others, or hack or DDoS them. You may not share sexually explicit information.
Pornography of any kind should not, and will not, be shared on Whittr.

You may not share content that glorifies or promotes suicide or self-harm, including any encouragement to others to cut themselves, or embrace eating disorders such as anorexia or bulimia. You may not share images of sadistic gore or animal cruelty.

You may not use Whittr for the organization, promotion, or support of violent extremism.

You may not create or be involved in a chat that sells or facilitates the sales of prohibited or potentially dangerous goods.
This includes firearms, ammunition, drugs, and controlled substances.

You may not promote, distribute, or provide access to content involving the hacking, cracking, or distribution of pirated software or stolen accounts.
This includes sharing or selling cheats or hacks that may negatively affect others in multiplayer games.

In general, you should not promote, encourage or engage in any illegal behavior.
